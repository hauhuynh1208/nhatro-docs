## Header

Title: Seller creates a replacement request
ID: US-014
Priority: Must
Actor: seller
As a: seller
I want: record a meter replacement for a buyer by entering the old and new meter readings
So that: the system has the data needed to correctly calculate consumption across the old and new meter

## Business Value

When a buyer's electricity or water meter is physically replaced, the consumption calculation must account for readings from both the old and the new meter. Recording the replacement with its readings ensures billing remains accurate after a meter swap. Without this, consumption would be over- or under-counted for the billing period of the replacement.

## Preconditions

- Seller is logged in
- At least one buyer exists for this seller

## Trigger

- Seller taps "Add Replacement Request" on the replacement request list screen

## Acceptance Criteria

> Each criterion must be testable and written from the user's or system's observable perspective — no API details.

1. Tapping "Add Replacement Request" opens a creation form.
2. The form includes the following fields:
   - **Buyer** (required): select from the seller's buyers.
   - **Type** (required): electricity or water.
   - **Old reading** (required): the physical meter reading at the time of the swap (value x).
   - **New reading** (required): the initial reading of the replacement meter after installation (value a).
3. Submitting the form with any required field empty shows a validation error on that field.
4. Submitting with a non-positive value for old reading or new reading shows a validation error on that field.
5. On successful submission, the new replacement request appears at the top of the replacement request list with the correct buyer name, type, old reading, new reading, and creation date.

## Notes

- The value y (the buyer's current reading on app at the time of the swap) is not stored in the replacement request. It is supplied implicitly by the old usage record when the seller applies the replacement in a future billing calculation, using the formula: `(b - a) + (x - y)`, where b is the new usage record reading, a is the new meter initial reading, x is the old meter physical reading at swap, and y is the old usage record reading at swap.
